Drowning in 2025

15 Dec 2025

Searching for yourself in 2025 - 2026

Many of us live in search of our true selves - to this very moment, I wonder where I’m going, what I’ll do, what the world has in store for me, for my wife, for our family.

We live in a time of uncertainty. Now more than ever, the fabric of the society that we live in feels to be bursting at the seams. This feeling is inextricable [is that the right word?] from the steady march forward of progress, of technology and social media.

It’s in this environment that we find ourselves searching for meaning in the chaos, to understand what we’re put on this Earth to do, to care for our family, to find love and kinship.

We are drowning.

Every single day I see and hear things that make me doubt whether the world is a suitable place to live in, let alone to bring a new generation of children into.

But at the same time, we live in an era of miracles, of advancement in every field. In many ways, the fantasies of the past century fail to meet the reality of the world that we live in now.

Where does this leave us

While uncertainty is nothing new to the human experience, this brand of it feels more existential. On August 9th, 1945, in the death throws of the second World War in human history, the United States of America detonated two atomic bombs over the cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki in Japan. In the same war, we also saw the Holocaust, in which millions were massacred in state-sanctioned execution.

In total in World War Two, the death count is considered to be between 70 and 85 million people, with the majority being civilian men, women and children.

In the midst of this and inumerable other traumatic and violent tragedies, our collective consciousnesses were brought closer together than ever before through rapid advancement in telecommunication technology.

We feel the pain of our fellow humans from around the world.

Between us and them lay entities with their own agendas that facilitate our connection.

All of these things, all of this context, cannot be ignored. It is all relevant to all of our lives, even now.

But ultimately, there is the individual

You and I, reader. Wherever and however human consciousness was formed, that is what all of our collective experience ultimately resolves to - the individual. We have no alternative experience to this.

And the experience of the individual now - in 2025/2026 - is not as harmonious as in the fantasy of a century ago.

We are still uncertain. We are still afraid.

A way forward

The individual created the world that we live in. The individual may also create the world in which we will live. This may not include the spheres of influence created by the current order - it may involve entirely new ones, perhaps ideas that we can’t even conceptualize.

The search for self in 2025/2026 is ladden with obstacles, with noise. But if continue to search, perhaps a new paradigm will emerge.

Perhaps there exists an interconectedness that resolves differences rather than exacerabtes them.

Perhaps there’s a future in which our technology works for us, rather than against us.

It is for us to decide. It is a future for us to dare to imagine, for us to create.

For the individual created the world in which we live, so we must be able to imagine one in which we continue to live more happily.
